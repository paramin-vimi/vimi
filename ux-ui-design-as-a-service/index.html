Choose an engagement that fits your budget.

When you choose us as your design provider, you have the option to hire us for project work, or on an ongoing retainer basis. In both models we supply a brief template and capability checklist to help you articulate your goals and align internal stakeholders. A well-defined brief avoids miscommunication and wasted spend.

Fixed-Scope Projects

Ideal when you have a clear brief and need certainty on budget and schedule. We prepare a detailed scope-of-work and provide a fixed cost. This model suits upgrades of existing platforms, discrete UX research engagements or defined UI deliverables (e.g. “Give me 3 options for this screen in our app”).

Retainer Partnership

Recommended for exploratory or long-term initiatives where requirements may evolve. How Does It Work?

Proven processes and timelines.

We follow a structured delivery workflow refined over 15 years. Each phase is designed to maximise ROI and minimise risk.

  1. 1

    Introduction, Planning and Outline

    Within 2 weeks of your brief

    We meet to understand your objectives, constraints and benchmarks for success. You receive a discovery summary, proposed team structure and a clear timeline.

    Goals

    • Understand your business and design objectives
    • Identify current situation and the benchmarks for success
    • Clarify what results will make a meaningful impact for your organization
    • Establish initial rapport and buy-in with your stakeholders
    • Introduce our team & working methodology

    Deliverables

    • Discovery summary brief
    • Plan summary & schedule for your UX / UI project
    • Proposal
  2. 2

    Setting Up

    1 – 2 days

    After your requirements are clarified and agreed, and contracting and payments are sorted, we’ll set up the shared accounts and repositories necessary to support your project.

    Goals

    • Create the shared working environment: drive folders, Smart Task project, Figma account, etc
    • Introduce stakeholders to the project’s agreed governance and work processes
    • Collate briefing materials and artefacts in the shared project repository

    Deliverables

    • Shared drive folder containing all existing project artefacts
    • User accounts as needed
    • Shared project on the agreed project management tool
  3. 3

    UX / UI Design

    As agreed during estimation

    Once you approve the work plan, and our team has access to your materials, we’ll start working on your project. We use weekly sprints for review and task assignment. If your project has a steering committee that approves works, we’d like to meet them once every two weeks for catch-up and approvals.

    Goals

    • Designers create and develop project artefacts matching the brief, agreed SOW, and process
    • Steering committee engagement and approvals
    • Full project transparency and visibility for your team at all times

    Deliverables — depending on the scope of work this may include:

    • Choice architecture
    • Process diagrams
    • Personas empathy maps
    • User stories
    • Visual designs
    • User interface design for iOS / Android / Desktop / Kiosk / Etc.
    • Design process documentation
  4. 4

    UX / UI Project Handover

    2 – 5 days, depending on scope

    When it’s time to handover the project to your team, we’re happy to offer training and documentation as needed. Alternatively, you can retain us as your UX / UI partner.

    Goals

    Enable smooth delivery and handover of design artefacts and project materials to your team(s).

    Deliverables — depending on the scope of work this may include:

    • Project documentation & change logs
    • Training session

FAQ

Questions? We have answers.

Straight answers to the questions B2B buyers actually ask about UX/UI design.

How is VIMI's UX/UI service different from hiring an in-house designer?

VIMI’s UX/UI service gives you senior-level design capability without the fixed cost, recruitment time, or retention risk of a full-time hire. You can expect to reduce designer costs by 49–53%, while gaining access to a multi-disciplinary team that includes UX researchers, interaction designers, visual designers, and UX writers. The service scales up or down month-to-month in line with your project pipeline.

How do you measure the ROI of UX/UI work?

During discovery we’ll define agreed benchmarks for improvement (e.g. conversion rate, task completion rate, time-on-task, etc.) Improvement will be measured in comparison to existing baselines measured by heatmapping and engagement tracking tools.

Which platforms and tools do you design in?

Our designers work primarily in Figma and deliver production-ready specifications for iOS, Android, web, and kiosk platforms. If your team uses a specific tool or has existing design files, we adapt to your environment rather than require migration.

Can you work with our existing brand guidelines, design system, or tech stack?

Yes. Most of our engagements extend existing assets rather than replace them. We audit what you have at the start of the project, identify gaps or inconsistencies, and work within your constraints. Where we recommend changes, we document the commercial rationale and implementation cost so your team can decide.

Who from VIMI will work on our project?

Your project is chaired by one of VIMI’s managing partners and coordinated day-to-day by a dedicated project manager. Depending on scope, your delivery team may include UX researchers, interaction designers, visual designers, UX writers, and information architects. Weekly check-ins are standard; monthly strategic reviews include a managing partner.

What happens after the designs are delivered?

We offer post-handover support on a retainer basis for teams that need ongoing design capacity, user testing during rollout, or iteration based on live-usage data.
