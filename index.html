FAQ

Questions? We have answers.

Straight answers to the questions B2B buyers actually ask.

What types of organizations does Vimi typically work with?

Vimi specializes in B2B organizations that sell complex and high-value solutions — manufacturers, industrial operators, infrastructure firms, financial and professional services, and technology platforms. Engagements are most effective for organizations with multi-stakeholder buying committees, sales cycles of three to twelve months, and regional or cross-border operations.

How does Vimi structure pricing and engagement terms?

Vimi offers you the choice of two engagement models – When your brief is well-defined and you need budget and timeline certainty, we engage based on a fixed-scope basis. Typical examples are website rebuilds, brand identity engagements, or defined scopes of UX-UI design. When your goal is ongoing UX-UI support or marketing execution that evolves with commercial priorities, we engage based on a monthly retainer.

How is Vimi different from a typical digital agency?

Vimi operates on a delivery model built for B2B buyers who require execution discipline, not creative experimentation. This includes defined monthly scopes, weekly account coordination, monthly performance reviews, managing-partner oversight on every account, and reporting tied to commercial outcomes such as pipeline contribution, cost per qualified lead, and conversion rates. As our client, you retain full visibility into what is being delivered for you, why, and how it's performing.

How does Vimi measure and report on commercial outcomes?

Every engagement begins with a discovery and benchmarking phase that establishes baseline metrics — website traffic, inquiry volume, conversion rates, search visibility, and pipeline contribution where attribution is available. Reporting is delivered monthly against agreed benchmarks, with regular reviews that connect marketing activity to commercial outcomes.

Who is accountable for delivery on our account?

Every Vimi account is led by a dedicated project or account lead who serves as your primary point of contact, coordinates the specialist delivery team, and reports to you weekly on progress. Senior oversight is provided by a managing partner who joins monthly review sessions, remains copied on key communications, and holds final accountability for delivery quality.
